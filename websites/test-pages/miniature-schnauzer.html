A Rich Heritage

Origins & History

Developed in late 19th century Germany, the Miniature Schnauzer was bred from the Standard Schnauzer mixed with smaller breeds like the Affenpinscher and Miniature Poodle. Originally, they served as efficient ratting dogs on farms, prized for their courage and small size which allowed them to navigate tight spaces.

Exceptional Temperament

Miniature Schnauzers are described as spirited, alert, and obedient. They are intelligent and willing to please, making them excellent watchdogs. While they can be aloof with strangers, they are incredibly loyal to their families.

Learn More

Size & Physicality

Standing typically 12 to 14 inches (30–35 cm) tall and weighing between 9 and 18 pounds (4–8 kg), they are a bridge between toy breeds and larger working dogs. Their iconic look includes a wiry coat, rectangular head, and expressive eyebrows and beard.
